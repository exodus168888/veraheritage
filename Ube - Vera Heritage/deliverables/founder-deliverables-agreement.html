Prepared for Marc and Chris May 2026
Operating Principle

Equity should match risk, time, accountability and measurable output.

The cleanest arrangement is to separate ownership, time commitment and deliverables. Marc’s 60% should carry full-time execution pressure. Chris’s 20% should carry part-time but high-leverage technical, product and documentation responsibility. The investor’s 20% should be tied to actual capital and agreed support.

Vera Heritage Founder Deliverables Framework01
Marc Deliverables

Marc owns the full-time commercial engine: capital, supply, buyers and execution.

Marc

Investor fundraising

  • Prepare and lead investor pitches.
  • Maintain investor pipeline with name, status, next step and probability.
  • Lead negotiations with the 20% investor and coordinate closing with legal/accounting support.
  • Send weekly fundraising report to Chris and the investor, once onboarded.
Target: 30 qualified leads, 10 serious conversations, 3 term-sheet-level discussions, 1 signed agreement.
Marc

Farmer and raw ube supply

  • Identify farmer cooperatives, farms, consolidators and landowners.
  • Build supplier database with location, variety, hectares, harvest timing, volume, price and contact person.
  • Secure initial supply commitments or MOUs.
  • Organize farm visits and verify supply claims.
Target: 10 supplier leads, 5 verified visits, 3 supplier MOUs, 5-10 MT raw ube equivalent identified.
Marc

Processing partner search

  • Find existing ube powder processors, dehydrators, millers, freeze-dry or spray-dry partners.
  • Request quotations for small-batch sample production.
  • Compare pricing, MOQ, certifications, lead time and packaging capability.
  • Coordinate raw material logistics from farm to processor.
Target: 5 processor leads, 3 formal quotes, 1 selected pilot processor, 1 completed test batch.
Marc

Buyer and distributor development

  • Build buyer list for US, Europe, Middle East and local institutional buyers.
  • Contact importers, Filipino distributors, specialty cafes, bakeries, ice cream makers, beverage brands and ingredient buyers.
  • Pitch samples and collect feedback on flavor, color, moisture, packaging, pricing and MOQ.
  • Secure LOIs, sample requests or trial orders.
Target: 100 leads, 30 buyer conversations, 10 sample requests, 3 LOIs or pilot purchase discussions.
Vera Heritage Founder Deliverables Framework02
Marc Deliverables Continued

Local raw ube trading can create early cash flow, but it needs discipline.

Cash Flow Track

Local raw ube trading

  • Source raw ube locally.
  • Find local buyers: processors, bakeries, halaya makers, traders, restaurants and food manufacturers.
  • Track buying price, transport cost, shrinkage, spoilage, selling price and margin.
  • Create weekly trading P&L.
  • Avoid speculative inventory without confirmed buyers.

Target: first trading transaction within 30-60 days, with agreed minimum gross margin, for example 15-25%.

Ground Execution

Operations management

  • Weekly field updates.
  • Supplier and buyer meeting reports.
  • Logistics coordination.
  • Government, LGU and cooperative relationship building.
  • Basic manpower coordination when needed.

Rule: Marc is the accountable operator because his 60% share is based on full-time leadership.

Trading should support the export strategy, not distract from it. Every local raw ube transaction should teach the business something about price, quality, seasonality, spoilage, farmer reliability or buyer demand.
Trading Rule Purpose Owner
No uncommitted bulk inventory Prevents spoilage and cash traps. Marc
Written transaction P&L Shows whether trading is really profitable after transport and shrinkage. Marc, reviewed by Chris
Supplier quality notes Creates early data for future export supply. Marc collects, Chris stores
Cash reinvestment policy Clarifies whether profits fund samples, operations or founder distributions. Joint decision

Vera Heritage Founder Deliverables Framework06
First 90 Days

The first phase should produce proof, not just plans.

Days 1-30 | Foundation

Decide and build

  • Marc: supplier list, cooperative meetings, processor leads, investor pipeline, local raw ube buyer search.
  • Chris: website structure, product spec draft, compliance checklist, CRM/data room, packaging direction.
  • Joint: finalize flagship, sample budget, local trading rules and written founder deliverables.
Days 31-60 | Proof

Make and test

  • Marc: supplier MOUs, pilot processor, buyer outreach, investor pitches, first local trading transaction if profitable.
  • Chris: live website, buyer materials, packaging mockup, shipment docs, batch/traceability system.
  • Joint: produce first sample batch or secure third-party powder sample under Vera branding.
Days 61-90 | Validation

Convert interest

  • Marc: LOIs or paid sample orders, investor commitment, expanded supplier commitments, better processing terms.
  • Chris: lab testing package, refined specs, investor data room, improved website and outreach funnel.
  • Joint: decide toll processing, raw trading priority, own processing plan and 12-month budget.
90-Day Success Means Evidence
Vera can access real ube supply. Signed MOUs, farm visit records, supplier database and price/volume map.
Vera can produce or source buyer-ready samples. Sample kit, packaging, product spec, processor quote and testing path.
Vera can reach buyers. CRM, outreach records, sample requests, feedback, LOIs or paid trials.
Vera can raise responsibly. Investor pipeline, data room, use of funds and milestone-based close.
Vera Heritage Founder Deliverables Framework07
Founder Protection

Put the expectations in writing before money, farmers and buyers are involved.

Recommended Clauses
  • Founder vesting schedule for Marc and Chris.
  • What happens if Marc stops being full-time.
  • What happens if Chris cannot contribute part-time.
  • What counts as acceptable weekly/monthly deliverables.
  • Who has final decision rights.
  • Spending approval limits.
  • When salaries begin, if cash flow allows.
  • Whether local trading profits are reinvested or distributed.
  • Whether equity changes if responsibilities materially change.
Simple Working Rule

Marc’s 60% is tied to full-time CEO execution.

Chris’s 20% is tied to part-time product, systems, compliance and technical marketing contribution.

Investor’s 20% depends on actual capital injection and agreed support terms.

If either founder materially stops contributing before an agreed period, unvested shares should return to the company.

Equity conversations are uncomfortable now, but they are much cheaper before there are purchase orders, investor money, farmer commitments and family expectations attached to the company.
