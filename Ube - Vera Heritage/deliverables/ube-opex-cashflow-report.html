Ube Business Opex, Cash Flow & Product Development Budget

A lean first-year operating plan for starting with local raw ube / ube powder trading, while building a buyer-ready export-grade ube powder product.

First 3 Months
Trade + learn

Use local trading to generate gross profit, validate suppliers, and learn quality/logistics.

Months 3-6
Build proof

Develop samples, packaging, specs, lab testing, documentation and buyer materials.

Months 7-12
Pilot export

Move toward paid pilots, distributor validation, repeat orders and disciplined scale.

Prepared for Marc + Chris May 2026 | Philippine peso estimates
Executive Summary

The business can start lean, but trading will not fully fund product development by itself.

Recommended base plan: prepare around ₱2.1M-₱2.4M cash requirement for the first six months, including product development, testing and lean operating costs, plus ₱300k-₱600k separate revolving trading capital. For Year 1, plan around ₱3.5M-₱4.8M total funding capacity before meaningful export reorders.

Operating Phases

Run two tracks at the same time: trading for cash/learning, product development for export value.

Months 1-3

Local Trading + Supplier Learning

  • Trade raw ube locally only against identified buyers.
  • Test small powder resale if reliable supplier exists.
  • Build supplier price, quality and seasonality database.
  • Keep fixed opex very low.
Months 2-6

Product Development

  • Buy raw ube for trials.
  • Pay toll processor sample fees.
  • Develop packaging sizes.
  • Run lab testing and build documentation.
Months 6-12

Buyer Pilots + Export Readiness

  • Send sample kits to US/EU/GCC buyers.
  • Close paid pilot orders.
  • Validate landed cost and reorder conditions.
  • Decide whether to deepen toll processing or plan facility capex.
The key management rule: trading should never consume all attention. It should finance learning and relationships while the higher-value export powder product is being built.

Do

Trade with controls

  • Pre-sell or identify buyer before purchase.
  • Grade by size, defects, color and solids.
  • Record farm source, kg bought, shrinkage and selling price.
  • Collect cash quickly; avoid long receivables.
Avoid

Speculative inventory

  • Do not hold raw ube without buyer.
  • Do not let trading consume product-development time.
  • Do not count inventory float as profit.
  • Do not extend credit to weak buyers.
Base expectation: local raw trading may offset ₱80k-₱150k of costs in the first three months if execution is good, but it should not be expected to fully fund the product-development plan.

Cash Controls

Use simple rules so the business does not accidentally become expensive.

Monthly Management Pack

Must be updated every month

  • Cash balance and runway.
  • Trading inventory and receivables.
  • Trading gross profit by transaction.
  • Product-development spend vs budget.
  • Buyer pipeline: samples sent, feedback, paid pilots.
  • Supplier pipeline: price, quality, volume, reliability.
Spending Approval Rules

Protect the cash

  • Any single spend above ₱25k requires both founders to approve.
  • Inventory buys require identified buyer or written trading thesis.
  • No custom packaging run above ₱100k without buyer feedback.
  • No full-time hire without 3 months of visible workload.
  • No plant capex without repeat orders and yield/cost proof.
